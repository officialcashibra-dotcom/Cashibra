What is Income Tax?

Income tax is money people pay to the government from the income they earn (salary, freelancing, interest, etc.). Governments use taxes for services like schools, roads and hospitals.

Quick idea: If you earn ₹50,000 a year, and the tax rate on your bracket is 10%, you pay ₹5,000 tax (before considering deductions).

Terms you should know

Gross Income: All money you received before any deductions.
Deductions: Amounts subtracted from gross income — e.g. standard deduction, student allowances, some investments.
Taxable Income: Gross income minus deductions. Tax is calculated on this number.

A simple progressive tax example

To keep the lesson clear we use a simple slab system (example only — change to real country slabs if needed):

  • 0% on income up to ₹2,50,000
  • 5% on income between ₹2,50,001 — ₹5,00,000
  • 10% on income between ₹5,00,001 — ₹10,00,000
  • 15% on income above ₹10,00,000
Note: This is an educational calculator with example slabs. Use real local slabs for precise tax planning.

How tax is calculated (step-by-step)

1) Start with Gross Income → 2) Subtract Deductions → 3) Apply slab rates to the taxable income in pieces → 4) Add health/education cess if applicable (not shown in this simple example).
